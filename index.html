  • What is Chess?
  • Chess is a strategic board game for two players where the goal is to checkmate the opponent's king. It's a classic game of intellectual engagement and planning, with a long and rich history. Each player controls 16 pieces, each with unique movement patterns, and the objective is to capture or block the opponent's king. /li>
  • Benefits of playing Chess:
  • Chess offers numerous cognitive and social benefits. It enhances memory, improves focus and concentration, boosts problem-solving skills, and strengthens strategic planning abilities. Playing chess also cultivates creativity, improves logical thinking, and can help with managing ADHD symptoms. Furthermore, chess promotes self-confidence, respect for others, and empathy.
  • Who invented the game?
  • Chess, as we know it, is the result of a gradual evolution rather than a single invention. The game's origins are typically traced back to India, where a game called chaturanga is believed to have been played around the 7th century CE. This game is considered the ancestor of modern chess. While no single person can be definitively named as the inventor, India is widely credited as the birthplace of the game.
